Item 8.01  Other Events.


On July 2, 2019, AT&T Inc. (“AT&T”, “we” or “the Company”) provided information regarding certain second-quarter transactions.

In June 2019, we amended, extended and increased our March 2019 revolving agreement to transfer certain receivables from our WarnerMedia business to various financial institutions in exchange for an additional $2.6 billion net cash proceeds during the second quarter. The agreement will now expire in June 2020 and the limit was increased to $4.0 billion.

On June 27, 2019, we prepaid a total of $2.6 billion of long-term debt obligations comprised of:
●
$1.5 billion of the $2.25 billion of the syndicated term loan agreement with The Bank of Nova Scotia, as administrative agent. At June 30, 2019, there was $750 million advances outstanding.
●
$0.5 billion of the $3.55 billion November 2018 term loan credit agreement with Bank of America, N.A. as agent. At June 30, 2019, there was approximately $3.0 billion outstanding under this agreement.
●
$0.6 billion under a July 2017 credit agreement with Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce, as administrative agent. This agreement was paid in full and was terminated.
